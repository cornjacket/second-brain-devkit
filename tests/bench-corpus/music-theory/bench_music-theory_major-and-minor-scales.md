# Major and Minor Scales

A scale is an ordered collection of pitches spanning an octave, and the major scale is built from the interval pattern whole, whole, half, whole, whole, whole, half, giving the familiar do-re-mi sound anchored on the tonic. The natural minor scale, or Aeolian mode, follows whole, half, whole, whole, half, whole, whole, lowering the third, sixth, and seventh scale degrees relative to the parallel major. Two derived forms adjust the upper tetrachord: the harmonic minor raises the seventh to create a leading tone and its striking augmented second, while the melodic minor raises both the sixth and seventh ascending and reverts to natural minor descending. Each scale degree carries a functional name, tonic, supertonic, mediant, subdominant, dominant, submediant, and leading tone, and these degrees supply the raw material for melody, key, and diatonic harmony throughout tonal music.
