# Voice Leading

Voice leading is the art of connecting successive chords by moving each of the individual melodic lines, or voices, as smoothly and independently as possible, treating harmony as the intersection of several simultaneous melodies. The guiding principles favor stepwise or common-tone motion over large leaps, resolving tendency tones such as the leading tone up to the tonic and chordal sevenths down by step. Classical practice forbids parallel perfect fifths and octaves between voices because they collapse the sense of independence, and it manages the four voices, soprano, alto, tenor, and bass, within comfortable ranges and proper spacing. Contrary and oblique motion between the outer voices produce the strongest textures, while doubling choices avoid doubling the leading tone. Good voice leading lets a progression sound inevitable, because each line traces a coherent path, and it explains why certain inversions and chord spacings feel more fluent than their alternatives.
