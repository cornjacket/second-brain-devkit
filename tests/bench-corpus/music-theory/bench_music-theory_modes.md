# Modes

The diatonic modes are seven scales obtained by starting the major-scale pattern on each of its successive degrees, so that the same collection of pitches yields differing interval orders and distinct tonal colors. Ionian is identical to the major scale, Aeolian to the natural minor, and between them lie Dorian with its raised sixth, Phrygian with its lowered second, Lydian with its raised fourth, Mixolydian with its lowered seventh, and Locrian with its diminished fifth and unstable tonic. Each mode is classified as major or minor by the quality of its third, and its characteristic note, the single degree that distinguishes it from the parallel major or minor, gives the mode its flavor. Modes underpin much folk, jazz, and modal composition, where a static drone or vamp replaces functional cadence, letting the ear absorb the scale's unique character rather than a dominant-to-tonic pull.
