# Tension and Resolution

Tension and resolution is the fundamental expressive engine of tonal music, the alternation of unstable, dissonant sonorities that create expectation with stable, consonant ones that satisfy it. Dissonant intervals such as the tritone, the minor second, and the chordal seventh generate harmonic tension, and tendency tones like the leading tone pull upward to the tonic while the seventh of a dominant chord resolves downward by step. Non-chord tones, suspensions, appoggiaturas, and unresolved dominants heighten anticipation, and the delay of an expected cadence prolongs the listener's sense of striving toward rest. Resolution arrives when these active pitches move to chord tones and the harmony returns to the tonic, releasing accumulated tension. Composers pace this ebb and flow across phrases to shape emotional contour, using dissonance treatment, cadential preparation, and the strategic withholding of the tonic to keep the ear engaged until closure finally grants repose.
