# Notation on the Staff

Staff notation represents pitch and duration on a grid of five lines and four spaces, where a note's vertical placement fixes its pitch and a clef anchors that placement to specific letter names. The treble clef curls around the G line, the bass clef frames the F line, and the movable C clefs place middle C on a chosen line for alto and tenor voices. Ledger lines extend the staff above or below for notes beyond its range, while accidentals, the sharp, flat, and natural signs, raise or lower a pitch by a half step for the remainder of the measure. Notehead shape and stems, flags, and beams encode rhythmic value, and rests mark measured silence. The grand staff joins treble and bass with a brace for keyboard music, and a system aligns multiple staves so that clefs, key signatures, time signatures, and barlines coordinate every part in vertical alignment.
