# Musical Form

Musical form is the large-scale architecture of a composition, the arrangement of phrases, sections, and themes into a coherent structure that listeners follow through repetition, contrast, and return. Small forms label sections with letters, so binary form pairs an A section with a contrasting B, and ternary form frames a contrasting middle with a returning A to give ABA symmetry. Rounded binary and thirty-two-bar song form elaborate these templates, while the rondo alternates a recurring refrain with episodes as ABACA. Sonata form organizes a movement into exposition, development, and recapitulation, presenting a tonic and a contrasting key area, fragmenting the themes through modulation, then resolving everything back in the home key. Theme and variations restate a subject in successively transformed guises, and through-composed music avoids literal repetition. Cadences, key relationships, and motivic development articulate these divisions, letting form shape the listener's expectation across an entire piece.
