# Key Signatures

A key signature is the group of sharps or flats placed at the beginning of the staff, just after the clef, indicating which pitches are consistently altered throughout a piece and thereby naming its key. Sharps accrue in the fixed order F, C, G, D, A, E, B, and flats in the reverse order B, E, A, D, G, C, F, each new accidental added a perfect fifth away in the sharp direction or a perfect fourth in the flat direction. To identify a sharp key, the last sharp sits on the leading tone, so the tonic lies a half step above it; for flat keys, the second-to-last flat names the tonic. Every major key shares its signature with a relative minor a minor third below, and enharmonic keys such as C-sharp and D-flat major describe the same seven pitches under different spellings.
