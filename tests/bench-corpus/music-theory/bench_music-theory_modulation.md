# Modulation

Modulation is the process of changing key within a piece, shifting the tonal center so that a new pitch becomes the tonic and a fresh key signature governs the harmony. The smoothest technique is the pivot chord, a chord common to both the old and new keys that is reinterpreted with a new Roman numeral before a cadence confirms the arrival. Closely related keys, those a fifth away or sharing the relative major or minor, allow gentle transitions, while chromatic modulation and common-tone modulation reach more distant keys through altered voice leading or a single sustained pitch. An enharmonic modulation reinterprets a chord such as a diminished seventh or German augmented sixth to swing abruptly into a remote tonality. Composers use modulation to build large-scale form, moving to the dominant in an exposition and returning home in the recapitulation, so that departure and return shape the listener's sense of tonal journey.
