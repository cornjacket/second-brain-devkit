# Cadences

A cadence is a harmonic and melodic formula that closes a phrase, functioning like punctuation to signal rest, continuation, or surprise within tonal music. The authentic cadence moves from dominant to tonic, and when both chords are in root position with the tonic in the soprano it becomes a perfect authentic cadence, the strongest possible conclusion. A half cadence pauses on the dominant, leaving the phrase harmonically open like a comma, while the plagal cadence progresses from subdominant to tonic, familiar as the church amen. The deceptive cadence sets up the dominant but resolves to the submediant instead, thwarting expectation and prolonging the music. Cadences interact with phrase structure, meter, and harmonic rhythm, typically landing on strong beats to reinforce closure. Composers weaken or strengthen them through inversion, soprano placement, and chord substitution to control how decisively a passage comes to rest.
