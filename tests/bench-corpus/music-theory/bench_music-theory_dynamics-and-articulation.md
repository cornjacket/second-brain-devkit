# Dynamics and Articulation

Dynamics and articulation are the notated markings that tell a performer how loudly to play and how to shape the attack and connection of individual notes. Dynamic levels run along a continuum from pianissimo through piano, mezzo-piano, mezzo-forte, forte, and fortissimo, with hairpin symbols indicating crescendo and diminuendo as the volume swells or fades, and accents such as sforzando marking sudden emphasis. Articulation governs how a note begins and ends: a slur binds notes into a smooth legato line, staccato dots detach and shorten them, tenuto marks sustain their full value, and the marcato wedge or accent stresses them sharply. Combined markings like staccatissimo, portato, and the fermata further refine phrasing, while breath marks and caesuras signal separation. Though these symbols address performance rather than pitch, notating them precisely is part of written music theory, since they encode the intended phrasing, weight, and expressive contour of the score.
