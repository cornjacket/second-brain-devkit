# Counterpoint

Counterpoint is the technique of combining two or more independent melodic lines into a coherent harmonic whole, prizing the horizontal integrity of each voice as much as the vertical intervals they form together. The species counterpoint pedagogy codified by Fux progresses from note-against-note first species through second-species two-notes-per-beat, third-species running notes, fourth-species suspensions, and fifth-species florid mixture. Consonances such as thirds, sixths, and perfect fifths anchor the texture, while dissonances are prepared and resolved as passing tones, neighbor tones, or suspensions on strong beats. Imitative counterpoint threads a subject through successive voices, reaching its height in the fugue with its exposition, answer, countersubject, and stretto. Invertible counterpoint lets lines exchange upper and lower positions, and canon binds voices in strict imitation at a fixed interval and delay. Throughout, contrary motion and independence of rhythm keep the interwoven melodies distinct yet harmonically consonant.
