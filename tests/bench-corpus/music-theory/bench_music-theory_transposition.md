# Transposition

Transposition is the operation of shifting every pitch in a passage up or down by a constant interval, preserving the melodic contour and harmonic relationships while relocating the music to a new key. Because all intervals remain intact, a melody transposed from C major to E-flat major sounds identical in shape, only higher or lower, and its Roman-numeral analysis is unchanged. Performers transpose to suit a singer's vocal range or the tessitura of an instrument, and transposing instruments such as the B-flat clarinet, the F horn, and the E-flat alto saxophone are written in one key but sound in another, requiring the reader to account for the instrument's transposition. Notating a transposition demands rewriting accidentals and choosing an appropriate key signature so enharmonic spelling stays legible. Diatonic transposition shifts within a scale by scale steps, whereas chromatic transposition moves by an exact interval, and both are staples of arranging and score preparation.
