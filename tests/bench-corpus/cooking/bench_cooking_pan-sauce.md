# Deglazing and pan sauces

A pan sauce turns the browned residue left from searing — the fond — into a glossy sauce in minutes, and it is one of the highest-leverage moves a cook can own. After searing meat, pour off excess fat, sweat a little minced shallot, then deglaze by adding wine or stock and scraping the fond loose with a wooden spoon as it bubbles. Reduce the liquid until it lightly coats a spoon, concentrating flavor, then pull the pan off the heat and mount it with cold butter, swirling so the fat emulsifies into a silky body rather than splitting. Finish with an acid — a squeeze of lemon or a splash of vinegar — and fresh herbs stirred in at the end. Season last, taste, and spoon it over the rested meat while both are still warm.
