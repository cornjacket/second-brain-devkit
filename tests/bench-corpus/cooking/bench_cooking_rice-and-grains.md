# Rice and grains

Cooking rice and grains well is about matching the method to the starch. For fluffy, separate grains, rinse long-grain rice to wash off surface starch, toast it briefly in fat as a pilaf, then cook it in a measured ratio of liquid, covered and undisturbed, and rest it off the heat so residual steam finishes and firms each grain. For creamy risotto you do the opposite — never rinse, and stir constantly while adding hot stock a ladle at a time so the released starch builds a silky suspension around an al dente core. Absorption, pilaf, and the pasta method — boil in abundant water and drain — each suit different grains, from quinoa to farro to polenta. Salt the cooking liquid so seasoning goes all the way through, and let cooked grains rest before fluffing so they do not turn gummy.
