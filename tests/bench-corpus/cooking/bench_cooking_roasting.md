# Roasting and carryover

Roasting cooks with dry, radiant oven heat, building a deep crust while the interior climbs gently to temperature. Bring the meat to room temperature first so it cooks evenly, and dry the surface hard so browning starts fast rather than steaming. A high initial blast sets color, then dropping the oven lets a large joint finish without a thick gray band under the crust; searing after a low roast — the reverse sear — is the modern alternative. Pull the roast several degrees below your target because carryover heat keeps climbing while it rests, and always rest it so the juices redistribute instead of flooding the board. A rack lifts the meat off its drippings for all-around convection, and those drippings become the foundation of a jus or gravy. A probe thermometer beats any timer.
