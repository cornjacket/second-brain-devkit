# Knife skills

Good knife work is the first thing a cook drills, because uniform cuts cook evenly and a plate reads clean. Teach the pinch grip — thumb and forefinger on the blade, not the handle — and the claw hand that curls the fingertips back so the knuckles guide the edge. The heel does heavy chopping, the tip does fine mincing, and a steady rocking motion keeps the blade in contact with the board. Standard cuts are worth memorizing: batonnet, julienne, brunoise, a chiffonade for herbs, and a rough dice for mirepoix. A sharp blade is safer than a dull one because it needs less force and won't skate off hard skins. Hone on a steel before each session, sharpen on a whetstone periodically, and cut on a damp-toweled board so it never slides.
