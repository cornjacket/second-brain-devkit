# Building a soup

Building a soup is an exercise in layering flavor from the ground up rather than boiling ingredients in water and hoping. Start by sweating aromatics in fat to build a savory base, often deepening it with a cook-out of tomato paste or a flour roux, then add a good stock rather than plain water so the liquid already carries body. Add ingredients in order of cooking time so nothing turns to mush, keep the pot at a gentle simmer to marry flavors without clouding the broth, and skim as needed. Puréed soups are blended smooth and often enriched with cream or an emulsified swirl of oil; brothy soups stay clear and are seasoned brightly right at the end. Acid and salt adjusted just before serving lift the whole bowl, and a contrasting garnish adds texture and freshness.
