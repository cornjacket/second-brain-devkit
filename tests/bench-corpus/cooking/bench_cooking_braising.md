# Braising tough cuts

Braising turns tough, collagen-rich cuts — chuck, shank, oxtail, short rib — into fork-tender meat through long, gentle, moist heat. Sear the surface hard first for a browned crust, then build a base by sweating aromatics and deglazing the fond with wine or stock. The liquid should come only partway up the meat, so the exposed top steams and bastes while the submerged part simmers. Hold a bare tremble around 300°F in a covered Dutch oven, never a rolling boil, or the muscle fibers seize and turn stringy. Over a couple of hours the collagen hydrolyzes into gelatin, which gives the sauce body and the meat its silky give. Rest the piece in its liquid, skim the fat off the top, then reduce the braise to a glossy nappe that coats a spoon.
