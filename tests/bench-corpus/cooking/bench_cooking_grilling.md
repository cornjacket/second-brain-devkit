# Grilling over live fire

Grilling cooks over live fire, and mastering it is mostly about managing heat zones and smoke. Build a two-zone fire — coals banked to one side — so you can sear over direct high heat then slide food to the indirect side to finish without charring. A clean, oiled, properly preheated grate is what keeps food from sticking and delivers clean bar marks. Fat dripping onto the coals causes flare-ups, so keep tongs and a cooler zone ready to dodge the flames. Thick cuts benefit from the reverse approach: cook gently on the indirect side, then sear last for color. Wood chips or chunks add smoke, and closing the lid turns the grill into a convection oven. Let the fire settle to the right ashen-gray coals before cooking, and rest grilled meat just as you would a roast.
