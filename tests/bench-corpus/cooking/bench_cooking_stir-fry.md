# Stir-frying and wok hei

Stir-frying cooks small, uniform pieces fast over ferocious heat in a wok, and its signature is wok hei — the smoky "breath of the wok" that only a screaming-hot surface produces. Success is mostly prep: everything cut evenly and lined up in order of cooking time, because once you start there is no pause. Heat the wok until it just smokes, add an oil with a high smoke point, then cook in small batches so the pan never cools and the food sears instead of stewing in its own released water. Aromatics like ginger, garlic, and scallion go in briefly so they perfume without scorching, and a premixed sauce is added at the end and tossed to glaze. Constant tossing keeps everything moving across the hottest zone, and a velveting cornstarch coat keeps thin meat tender.
