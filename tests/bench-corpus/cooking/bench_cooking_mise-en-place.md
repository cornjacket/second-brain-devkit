# Mise en place

Mise en place — "everything in its place" — is the discipline of prepping and organizing all ingredients and tools before the heat ever goes on, and professional kitchens run on it. You read the recipe through, then wash, peel, cut, measure, and arrange every component in small bowls in the order you will use them, so that once cooking starts you are executing rather than scrambling. It matters most for fast techniques like stir-frying or à la minute service, where there is no time to mince garlic mid-cook. Beyond ingredients it includes a clean station, a sharp knife, a damp towel to steady the board, and a bowl for scraps to keep the workspace clear. Good mise turns a chaotic cook into a calm, repeatable one, and it is the first habit any serious kitchen drills into a new hire.
