# Legal and Equitable Remedies

Remedies are the relief a court awards a prevailing party to redress a legal wrong, divided into the historic categories of law and equity. Legal remedies center on money damages: compensatory damages restore the plaintiff to the position occupied but for the wrong, comprising general and consequential losses; nominal damages vindicate a right where no substantial harm is shown; and punitive damages punish egregious, willful misconduct and deter its repetition. Equitable remedies, available where the legal remedy is inadequate, include the injunction commanding or forbidding conduct, specific performance compelling a contracting party to render the promised performance, rescission unwinding a transaction, and reformation correcting a mistaken writing. Restitution disgorges unjust enrichment, restoring to the claimant a benefit wrongfully retained by the defendant. The chancellor's discretion, the doctrines of laches and unclean hands, and the requirement of irreparable injury govern the availability of equitable relief that the reviewing court may grant.
