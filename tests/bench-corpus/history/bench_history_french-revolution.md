# The French Revolution

The French Revolution erupted in 1789 as fiscal crisis, famine, and resentment of the ancien régime forced Louis XVI to convene the Estates-General at Versailles. The Third Estate declared itself the National Assembly and swore the Tennis Court Oath, while Parisians stormed the Bastille on the fourteenth of July. The Assembly abolished feudal privileges and proclaimed the Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen. As the monarchy fell and the First Republic was declared, radical Jacobins led by Robespierre unleashed the Reign of Terror, sending the king, Marie Antoinette, and thousands to the guillotine. The Committee of Public Safety, sans-culottes, and revolutionary tribunals dominated until Robespierre's own execution in the Thermidorian Reaction of 1794. The unstable Directory followed, until Napoleon Bonaparte seized power in the coup of 18 Brumaire in 1799.
