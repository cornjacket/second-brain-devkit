# The Cold War

The Cold War was the geopolitical rivalry between the United States and the Soviet Union that dominated world affairs from 1945 until 1991. After the defeat of Nazi Germany, the wartime allies split Europe along Churchill's Iron Curtain, and the Truman Doctrine and Marshall Plan sought to contain Soviet communism. The Berlin Blockade, the founding of NATO and the Warsaw Pact, and the Korean War hardened the divide. The superpowers amassed nuclear arsenals under mutually assured destruction, confronting one another most perilously in the Cuban Missile Crisis of 1962. Proxy conflicts raged in Vietnam, Afghanistan, and across the decolonizing world, while the Space Race carried the contest beyond Earth. Détente eased tensions in the 1970s before renewed confrontation under Reagan. Mikhail Gorbachev's glasnost and perestroika, the fall of the Berlin Wall in 1989, and the Soviet collapse in 1991 ended the standoff.
