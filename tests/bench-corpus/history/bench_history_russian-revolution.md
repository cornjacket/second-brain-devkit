# The Russian Revolution of 1917

The Russian Revolution of 1917 toppled the Romanov dynasty and brought the Bolsheviks to power amid the catastrophe of the First World War. In the February Revolution, bread riots and mutinies in Petrograd forced the abdication of Tsar Nicholas II, replacing autocracy with a Provisional Government under Alexander Kerensky that shared authority with the Petrograd Soviet in a fragile dual power. Vladimir Lenin returned from Swiss exile in a sealed train, proclaiming the April Theses and the slogan peace, land, and bread. In the October Revolution, Bolshevik Red Guards led by Leon Trotsky stormed the Winter Palace and overthrew the Provisional Government. The new regime signed the Treaty of Brest-Litovsk with Germany and nationalized industry. A brutal civil war ensued between the Red Army and the White forces, ending in Bolshevik victory and the founding of the Soviet Union.
