# The Meiji Restoration

The Meiji Restoration of 1868 ended over two and a half centuries of Tokugawa shogunate rule and restored imperial authority under the young Emperor Meiji, propelling Japan into rapid modernization. The arrival of Commodore Matthew Perry's black ships in 1853 had forced the isolationist Bakufu to open ports, humiliating the shogunate and rallying samurai reformers from the Satsuma and Chōshū domains under the slogan sonnō jōi. After the Boshin War toppled the last shogun, Tokugawa Yoshinobu, the new oligarchs abolished the feudal han and the samurai class, promulgated the Charter Oath, and relocated the capital to Tokyo. They built railways, telegraphs, and modern factories, adopted a Prussian-inspired constitution and conscript army, and dispatched the Iwakura Mission abroad. This state-led industrialization transformed Japan into an industrial and military power that soon defeated Qing China and Tsarist Russia.
