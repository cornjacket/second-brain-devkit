# The American Civil War

The American Civil War raged from 1861 to 1865 between the Union and the eleven seceding Confederate states over slavery and its expansion into the western territories. After Abraham Lincoln's election and the firing on Fort Sumter, the Confederacy under President Jefferson Davis and General Robert E. Lee waged war against Union armies commanded eventually by Ulysses S. Grant and William Tecumseh Sherman. Bloody engagements at Bull Run, Shiloh, Antietam, Gettysburg, and Vicksburg cost hundreds of thousands of lives. Lincoln's Emancipation Proclamation of 1863 redefined the conflict as a struggle for abolition, and the Thirteenth Amendment followed. Sherman's march through Georgia devastated the Confederate heartland before Lee surrendered at Appomattox Court House in April 1865. Days later Lincoln was assassinated by John Wilkes Booth. The Union victory abolished slavery and inaugurated the contested era of Reconstruction across the defeated South.
