# Postwar Decolonization

Decolonization dismantled the European colonial empires across Asia and Africa in the decades following the Second World War, which had drained the imperial powers and discredited their claims to rule. In British India, the Indian National Congress under Mohandas Gandhi and Jawaharlal Nehru won independence in 1947 amid the bloody Partition that created Pakistan. France fought and lost brutal wars in Indochina, culminating at Dien Bien Phu in 1954, and in Algeria, where the FLN battled until 1962. In Ghana, Kwame Nkrumah led the first sub-Saharan colony to independence in 1957, and the 1960 Year of Africa saw seventeen new nations emerge. The Bandung Conference of 1955 gave voice to a non-aligned bloc. Independence movements, sometimes negotiated and sometimes won through armed insurgency, redrew the world map as scores of former colonies claimed sovereign statehood.
