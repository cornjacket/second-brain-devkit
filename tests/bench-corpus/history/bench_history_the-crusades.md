# The Crusades to the Holy Land

The Crusades were a series of papally sanctioned military expeditions launched after Pope Urban II's sermon at the Council of Clermont in 1095, calling Latin Christendom to reclaim Jerusalem from Seljuk Turkish control. Crusaders sewed the cross upon their tunics and marched east, capturing Antioch and storming Jerusalem in 1099 to establish the Crusader States, including the County of Edessa and the Kingdom of Jerusalem. Military orders such as the Knights Templar and Hospitallers garrisoned castles like Krak des Chevaliers. Saladin, the Ayyubid sultan, retook Jerusalem in 1187 after the Battle of Hattin, provoking the Third Crusade under Richard the Lionheart and Frederick Barbarossa. The Fourth Crusade infamously sacked Constantinople in 1204. Successive expeditions faltered until the Mamluks captured Acre in 1291, extinguishing the Latin foothold in the Levant.
