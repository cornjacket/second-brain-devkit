# The Roman Republic and Its Constitutional Order

Founded around 509 BCE after the expulsion of the Etruscan king Tarquinius Superbus, the Roman Republic governed through a mixed constitution balancing the Senate, the popular assemblies, and annually elected magistrates. Two consuls held imperium and commanded the legions, while praetors administered justice and quaestors managed the treasury. The Conflict of the Orders pitted plebeians against patricians, yielding the tribunes of the plebs, the Twelve Tables, and the Lex Hortensia. Rome expanded across Italy through the Latin and Samnite Wars, then defeated Carthage in the three Punic Wars, destroying it in 146 BCE. Figures like Cato the Elder, Scipio Africanus, and the reformer Gracchi brothers shaped the era. The Republic ultimately faltered amid the civil wars of Marius, Sulla, Pompey, and Julius Caesar, whose crossing of the Rubicon in 49 BCE presaged its collapse into empire.
