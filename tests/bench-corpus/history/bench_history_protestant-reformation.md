# The Protestant Reformation

The Protestant Reformation fractured Western Christendom after the Augustinian friar Martin Luther posted his Ninety-Five Theses at Wittenberg in 1517, protesting the sale of indulgences by the friar Johann Tetzel to finance Saint Peter's Basilica. Luther's doctrine of justification by faith alone and scriptura sola, disseminated through printed pamphlets, defied Pope Leo X and Emperor Charles V at the Diet of Worms in 1521. Reform spread as Huldrych Zwingli preached in Zurich and John Calvin established a theocratic Geneva grounded in predestination. In England, Henry VIII broke with Rome through the Act of Supremacy to found the Anglican Church. The Catholic Church answered with the Counter-Reformation, convening the Council of Trent, empowering the Jesuits under Ignatius of Loyola, and reasserting the sacraments. The ensuing confessional strife ignited wars of religion that ravaged Europe for over a century.
