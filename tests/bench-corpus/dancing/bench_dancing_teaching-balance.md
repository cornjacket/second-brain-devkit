# Teaching balance

Balance is trainable, and teachers build it as an active skill rather than a lucky knack. The lesson is that standing on one leg is not holding still but constantly, minutely adjusting, so students learn to pull up out of the standing leg, engage the core, and find a stacked vertical line from the foot through the head. Drill sustained balances — a passé relevé held a little longer each time — and cue awareness of the tripod of the foot and the micro-corrections at the ankle. Teach students to fix the eyes on a steady point and to lengthen upward rather than gripping down, because tension actually destroys balance. Removing the barre gradually forces the body to own its placement. Strong balance underpins clean turns and controlled adagio, where a wobble is unforgiving.
