# Teaching allegro and jumps

Allegro — the jumping vocabulary — is coached around one truth: a good jump is a good plié in both directions. Teach students to push through the whole foot into the floor to launch, fully stretch the legs, feet, and toes at the height of the jump, then land by rolling through the foot back into a soft, deep plié that absorbs the impact silently. Start small with sautés and changements to groove the takeoff and landing before adding beaten steps or big traveling jumps. Cue them to keep the upper body lifted and calm so the effort stays in the legs, and to use the airborne instant to show a clear, finished shape. Emphasize quiet landings, because noise means the plié is not cushioning and the joints are taking the shock instead.
