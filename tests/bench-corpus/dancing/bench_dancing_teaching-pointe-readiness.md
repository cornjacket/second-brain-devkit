# Teaching pointe readiness

Putting a student on pointe too early is a real injury risk, so teachers gate it behind readiness rather than age or eagerness. The prerequisites are years of training, enough ankle and foot strength to fully rise onto the box with a straight, supported leg, strong turnout and core control, and no rolling of the ankles. Coaches assess whether a student can hold a clean relevé and balance on demi-pointe before ever lacing up. Early pointe work happens at the barre in short doses — rises, échappés, and bourrées — with obsessive attention to pulling up out of the shoe rather than sinking into it. Proper fitting, and shoes darned and secured with ribbons and elastic, matter enormously. The goal is strength first, so the growing skeleton is protected while the feet learn the work.
