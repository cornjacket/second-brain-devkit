# Teaching stage presence

Stage presence is coachable, and teachers work on it so a technically strong dancer also reaches an audience. The core lesson is projecting energy outward and completing every movement past its obvious end, so the lines extend through the fingertips and the gaze carries to the back of the house instead of dropping to the floor. Teach students to dance "big" for the space, since movement that fills a studio can look small on a stage, and to use focus — where the eyes and face are directed — to draw attention and convey intention. Breath and a lifted, open posture read as confidence. Coaches also rehearse recovering invisibly from a slip and staying in character, so nothing breaks the performance. Presence is the difference between merely executing steps and commanding the stage.
