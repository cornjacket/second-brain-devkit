# Teaching musicality to dancers

Musicality is what separates steps done merely on time from dancing that seems to grow out of the music, and a teacher can build it deliberately through the body rather than through theory. Train students to let the sound change how they carry weight — to sink and broaden through a slow, sustained passage, then rebound crisply off a driving one — so timing lives in the muscles instead of the head. Coach the physical vocabulary of accent: where to suspend at the top of a movement and hang for an extra breath, where to release early and let momentum carry the step, where to attack sharply from the floor and where to let a gesture melt away. Have dancers feel syncopation as a shift of weight that lands between the obvious pulses. A dancer who merely marks the timing looks mechanical; one who lets the music move the body reads as alive.
