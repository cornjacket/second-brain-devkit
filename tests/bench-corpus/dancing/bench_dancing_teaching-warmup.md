# Warming up dancers

A proper warm-up prepares a dancer's body and focus, and teaching students to do it themselves is part of the training. The purpose is to raise the core temperature, lubricate the joints, and gradually recruit the muscles before demanding full range or force, which lowers injury risk and improves control. Coaches lead a progression from gentle, whole-body movement that raises the heart rate, into mobilizing the spine, hips, and ankles, and finally into dynamic movements that rehearse the patterns class will use. They steer students away from deep static stretching of a cold body, which can leave muscles slack and unready. A good warm-up also settles the mind and brings attention into the studio. Dancers learn to arrive early and prepare rather than walking in cold and forcing the first exercises.
