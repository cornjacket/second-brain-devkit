# Conditioning and injury prevention

Smart teachers treat conditioning and injury prevention as part of training, not an afterthought, because dancing demands extreme range under load. Cross-training builds the strength that technique alone does not — core stability, hip and foot strength, and balanced work for the muscles that oppose the ones dance overuses — so alignment holds when a dancer fatigues. Coaches teach proper landing mechanics, gradual increases in workload, and honest rest, since most dance injuries are overuse from repeating the same stress rather than dramatic accidents. They watch for warning signs like rolling ankles, gripping hips, or pain that gets pushed through, and they distinguish productive soreness from injury. Warming up before and cooling down after, plus adequate fuel and sleep, round it out. A durable dancer trains the whole body, not just the visible skills.
