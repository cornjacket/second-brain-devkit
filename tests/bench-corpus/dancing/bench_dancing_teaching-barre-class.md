# Structuring a barre class

Structuring a ballet class means building the body up in a deliberate order, and a teacher plans the barre as a warm-up that progresses from simple to complex. It opens with pliés to warm the joints and establish alignment, moves through tendus and dégagés to articulate the feet, then ronds de jambe for hip mobility, frappés and fondus for strength and control, and grand battements to open the range. Each exercise is given on both sides, and the teacher sets a clear order so the muscles are prepared before they are demanded. The barre provides support so students can concentrate on placement before the same material is repeated in the open floor — the centre — without it. A well-built barre leaves the body genuinely ready for turns and jumps.
