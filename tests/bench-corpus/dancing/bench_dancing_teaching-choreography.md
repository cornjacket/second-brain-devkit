# Teaching choreography retention

Learning and retaining choreography is a teachable skill, and a good teacher builds it deliberately rather than assuming students just remember. Movement is chunked into short sequences, named, and repeated so the body encodes it as muscle memory instead of a list of steps. Dancers mark — running the material at low energy, indicating steps with small gestures — to rehearse pathways, spacing, and order without exhausting themselves, then run it full out to test recall under fatigue. Teach students to note transitions and directions, to track their spacing relative to others, and to review the material mentally away from the studio. Facing changes and floor patterns are drilled separately from the steps. The goal is a dancer who can absorb material quickly and reproduce it cleanly, which is exactly what rehearsals and auditions demand.
