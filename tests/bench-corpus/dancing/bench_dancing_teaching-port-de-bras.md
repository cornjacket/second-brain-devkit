# Teaching port de bras

Port de bras — the carriage of the arms — is what makes dancing look finished, and beginners tend to neglect it while chasing footwork. Teach that the arms move from the back, initiated between the shoulder blades, so they float with a soft, supported curve rather than stiffening at the elbow or breaking at the wrist. Drill the basic positions and the pathways between them until they are smooth and unhurried, coordinating the arms with the breath and with épaulement — the subtle tilt of the head and shoulders that gives a pose its dimension. Cue students to keep the shoulders down and away from the ears and to let the fingertips lead and lengthen the line. Expressive, quiet arms turn a merely correct step into real dancing.
