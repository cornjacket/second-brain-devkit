# Teaching the plié

The plié — a smooth bend of the knees — is the first thing taught at the barre and the hidden engine of nearly every step, so it rewards patient coaching. Teach students to bend while keeping the heels down as long as possible in a demi-plié, the spine lengthened and the turnout held from the hips, so the knees track directly over the toes and never roll inward. Cue the descent and rise as one continuous, resisted motion rather than a bounce, because that control is what cushions every landing from a jump and powers every push into a turn. A grand plié continues until the heels release, keeping the seat from tucking under. Emphasize that a shallow, honest plié with true alignment beats a deep one that sacrifices the knees.
