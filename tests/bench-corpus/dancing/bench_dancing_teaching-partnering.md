# Teaching partnering

Partnering, or pas de deux, is a skill of two bodies sharing weight and timing, and it is taught with safety and communication first. Beginners learn supported balances — the partner's hands at the waist steadying a promenade or a pirouette — before any lifts, so trust and placement come before risk. Teach the supporting dancer to feel the other's center and to give steady, predictable support rather than yanking, and teach the supported dancer to hold their own core so they stay light and self-carrying, never dead weight. Lifts are broken down on the ground and spotted, with clear preparation cues so both dancers commit at the same instant. Emphasize eye contact and breath as the shared language of timing. Good partnering looks effortless precisely because the mechanics were drilled slowly and deliberately.
