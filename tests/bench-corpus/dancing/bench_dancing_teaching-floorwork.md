# Teaching floorwork

Floorwork — moving into, across, and out of the ground — is central to modern and contemporary technique, and teaching it means retraining students who were taught to stay upright. The key skills are getting down and up efficiently by spiraling through the spine and yielding with control rather than collapsing, and using momentum so the floor becomes a partner that gives energy back. Teach students to protect the joints: roll across the fleshy surfaces, avoid banging the knees and hips, and use the hands to modulate how weight arrives. Release-based approaches emphasize letting go of unnecessary tension so gravity does the work, while the core stays active to initiate and catch the movement. Slides, rolls, and rebounds are drilled until transitions are seamless and a dancer can pour weight to the ground and recover without visible effort.
