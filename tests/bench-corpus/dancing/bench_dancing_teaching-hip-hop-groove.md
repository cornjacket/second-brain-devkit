# Teaching hip-hop groove

Hip-hop technique starts from the groove — the relaxed, grounded bounce that lives in the knees and chest — and teaching it means loosening dancers trained to stay lifted and pulled up. The foundation is the bounce and the rock, a subtle up-or-down pulse through bent knees that keeps the body athletic and ready. From there teachers layer isolations, moving one body part — the chest, the head, the ribs — while the rest stays still, which demands real control. Cue students to stay low, heavy, and reactive, letting the movement settle into the floor rather than floating above it. Foundational styles like popping and locking have their own precise mechanics of muscle contraction and freeze. The through-line is a loose, confident, weighted quality that beginners reach only by relaxing and dropping their center.
