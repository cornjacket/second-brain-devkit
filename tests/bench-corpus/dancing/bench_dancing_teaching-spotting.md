# Teaching spotting for turns

Spotting is the technique that lets a dancer turn repeatedly without getting dizzy, and it must be taught explicitly because it is counterintuitive. The head stays facing a fixed point as long as possible while the body rotates, then whips around fast to re-find the same spot, so the eyes are still for most of the turn. Start students with slow quarter- and half-turns on two feet, drilling the sharp head snap before adding any speed or a relevé. Cue them to pick a spot at eye level and keep the whipping motion crisp rather than lazy, because a late head is what makes pirouettes wobble and travel across the floor. Layer spotting onto chaînés and piqué turns once the reflex is reliable, then finally onto multiple pirouettes with a strong vertical axis.
