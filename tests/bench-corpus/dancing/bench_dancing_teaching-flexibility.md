# Teaching flexibility safely

Flexibility is coached carefully because dancers need extreme range without sacrificing the strength and stability that make it usable and safe. Teachers favor dynamic, active stretching that lengthens muscles through motion as part of a warm-up, saving deep static holds for after class when the body is warm. The crucial idea taught is active flexibility — being able to lift and hold the leg high using your own muscles, not just drop into a high split with help — because range you cannot control offers no benefit and invites injury. Coaches discourage forcing, bouncing, or pushing a cold body, and they pair every gain in range with strengthening so the joints stay supported. Progress is slow and individual, bounded by each dancer's structure, and honest teachers respect that limit rather than chasing an extreme line.
