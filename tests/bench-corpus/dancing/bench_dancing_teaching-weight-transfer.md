# Teaching weight transfer

Nearly every step is really a transfer of weight, and teaching dancers to feel that is more useful than teaching shapes. Coaches drill simple walking, stepping, and shifting exercises where students consciously move their center of mass from one leg to the other, arriving fully over the standing leg before the next move. This is what makes traveling steps flow and turns launch cleanly, because a turn spins around a committed vertical axis, not a half-shifted one. Teach the difference between being centered over one leg and being caught between two, since that in-between is where balance and momentum leak away. Momentum from a well-timed weight shift powers jumps and lunges, so a dancer who understands weight moves efficiently and looks grounded rather than effortful and stuck between the feet.
