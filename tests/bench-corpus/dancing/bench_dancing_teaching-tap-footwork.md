# Teaching tap footwork

Tap technique is taught as making clear, distinct sound with the feet, so the coaching centers on articulation and weight rather than big shapes. Students learn to isolate the ankle and let the leg stay relaxed, striking the floor with the metal toe and heel taps to make separate sounds — the shuffle, flap, ball-change, and cramp roll each have a precise mechanic to drill slowly before any speed. Teach clarity first: every sound should be clean and distinct, which means controlling how much weight lands and lifting the foot crisply between strikes. Relaxed knees and a quiet, upright upper body let the sound come from the feet alone. Coaches build speed only gradually and layer combinations once the basic sounds are reliable, because muddy, blurred taps come from tension and dragging the feet.
