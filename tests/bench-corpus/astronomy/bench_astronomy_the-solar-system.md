# The Solar System

The Solar System comprises the Sun and everything gravitationally bound to it, including eight planets that orbit within the ecliptic plane along elliptical paths. The four inner terrestrial worlds, Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars, are rocky and dense, while the outer giants Jupiter and Saturn are gas giants and Uranus and Neptune are ice giants ringed by numerous moons. Between Mars and Jupiter lies the asteroid belt, and beyond Neptune stretches the Kuiper Belt of icy planetesimals, home to dwarf planets like Pluto and Eris, encircled far outward by the hypothesized Oort Cloud. Planetary orbits obey Keplerian mechanics, their periods lengthening with heliocentric distance. The whole system formed roughly 4.6 billion years ago from a collapsing molecular cloud that flattened into a protoplanetary disk, where dust grains accreted into planetesimals and ultimately into planets circling our star.
