# Comets and Asteroids

Comets and asteroids are the small rocky and icy bodies left over from the Solar System's formation. Asteroids are rocky and metallic remnants concentrated in the main belt between Mars and Jupiter, ranging from the dwarf planet Ceres down to boulder-sized fragments, their orbits shepherded by Jupiter's gravity into families and gaps. Comets are icy planetesimals from the frigid Kuiper Belt and distant Oort Cloud that, when nudged inward, develop a glowing coma of sublimating gas and dust as solar heating vaporizes their ices, trailing a dust tail and an ion tail that always point away from the Sun. Both classes preserve pristine material from the protoplanetary disk, making them time capsules of early Solar System chemistry. Spacecraft like Rosetta and OSIRIS-REx have rendezvoused with and sampled these bodies, and meteor showers occur when Earth plows through the debris streams comets leave along their orbits.
