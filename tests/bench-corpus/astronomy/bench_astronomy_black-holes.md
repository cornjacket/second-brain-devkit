# Black Holes

A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so intense that nothing, not even light, can escape once it crosses the event horizon. Stellar-mass black holes form when the most massive stars collapse at the end of their lives, while supermassive black holes millions to billions of times the Sun's mass lurk at the centers of galaxies, including Sagittarius A star at the heart of the Milky Way. According to general relativity, all the mass is crushed into a singularity, and the horizon radius scales with mass as the Schwarzschild radius. Matter spiraling inward forms a superheated accretion disk that radiates intense X-rays, and some black holes launch relativistic jets. The Event Horizon Telescope has imaged the shadow cast by the horizon, and merging black holes ripple spacetime with gravitational waves detected by observatories on Earth.
