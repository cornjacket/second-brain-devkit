# Nebulae

A nebula is an interstellar cloud of gas and dust, the raw material from which stars are born and the glowing debris that dying stars leave behind. Emission nebulae like the Orion Nebula shine as ultraviolet radiation from hot young stars ionizes surrounding hydrogen, causing it to glow crimson in a region astronomers call an HII region. Reflection nebulae scatter blue starlight off dust grains, while dark nebulae such as the Horsehead appear as opaque silhouettes blocking the light behind them. Within these stellar nurseries, gravity draws dense knots of gas into collapsing cores that become protostars. When a Sun-like star dies, it puffs off a glowing planetary nebula, and a massive star's supernova blast leaves an expanding supernova remnant like the Crab. The interstellar medium is thus continually recycled, its enriched gas gathering into new clouds that seed successive generations of stars.
