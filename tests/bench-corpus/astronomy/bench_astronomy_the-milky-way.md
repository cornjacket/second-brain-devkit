# The Milky Way

The Milky Way is the barred spiral galaxy that hosts our Solar System, a disk roughly a hundred thousand light-years across containing several hundred billion stars along with gas, dust, and a dominant halo of dark matter. Our Sun lies in the Orion Arm, about twenty-six thousand light-years from the galactic center, and orbits it once every 230 million years, a span sometimes called a galactic year. The luminous band arching across a dark night sky is our edge-on view through the crowded stellar disk, mottled by dark dust lanes that obscure the light behind them. At the galactic core sits the supermassive black hole Sagittarius A star, its presence betrayed by stars whipping around it at tremendous speeds. Surrounding the disk is a spherical halo studded with ancient globular clusters, and the Milky Way anchors the Local Group, gravitationally bound to Andromeda, with which it will merge in a few billion years.
