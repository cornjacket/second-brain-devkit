# Debt payoff: snowball versus avalanche

When a household juggles several balances, two popular strategies decide which debt to attack first while paying the minimum on the rest. The debt snowball orders your balances from smallest to largest and throws every spare dollar at the smallest first, delivering quick wins that build motivation as each account disappears; the psychological momentum keeps many people going even though it ignores interest rates. The debt avalanche instead targets the balance with the highest interest rate first, which is mathematically optimal because it minimizes the total interest you pay and clears the debt fastest overall. The right choice depends on temperament: if early victories keep you committed, snowball; if you are disciplined and want to save the most money, avalanche. Either way, list every card and loan with its rate and minimum, funnel all extra cash to the target account, and roll each freed-up payment onto the next debt until the household is debt-free.
