# Tracking your net worth

Net worth is the single number that captures a household's financial position: add up everything you own and subtract everything you owe. Your assets include cash in checking and savings, the balances in retirement and brokerage accounts, the market value of your home and car, while your liabilities cover the mortgage, student loans, auto loans, and credit card balances. The difference, which can be negative early in life, is your net worth, and watching it climb quarter after quarter is a better gauge of progress than your income alone. A rising number means you are saving, paying down debt, and letting investments compound; a stalled one signals lifestyle creep or debt eating your gains. Update a simple spreadsheet every few months, celebrate the milestone when assets first exceed liabilities, and use the trend rather than any single snapshot to steer your saving and spending decisions toward long-term wealth building.
