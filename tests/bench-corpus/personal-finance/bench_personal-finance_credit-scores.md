# Understanding your credit score

A credit score is a three-digit number, typically ranging from 300 to 850 on the FICO scale, that lenders use to gauge how reliably a household repays borrowed money. Five factors drive it: your payment history carries the most weight, so never missing a due date matters most, followed by your credit utilization, the share of available revolving credit you are actually using, which you want to keep below thirty percent. The length of your credit history, the mix of loans and cards you hold, and how many new accounts you have recently opened round out the formula. A higher score unlocks lower interest rates on mortgages, auto loans, and credit cards, saving a household real money over the life of a loan. Check your report yearly for errors, pay every bill on time, keep old accounts open, and let utilization stay low to nurture a strong score over time.
