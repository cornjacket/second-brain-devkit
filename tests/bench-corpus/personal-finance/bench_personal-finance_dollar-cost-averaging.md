# Dollar-cost averaging into the market

Dollar-cost averaging is the habit of investing a fixed amount of money on a regular schedule, say two hundred dollars every payday, regardless of whether the market is up or down. Because your fixed sum buys more shares when prices are low and fewer when prices are high, the average cost per share you pay smooths out over time, sparing you the impossible task of timing the market's peaks and valleys. For most households this happens automatically through a 401k contribution or a recurring brokerage transfer, turning investing into a boring, disciplined routine rather than an emotional gamble. The real power is behavioral: it keeps you buying steadily through scary downturns, exactly when panicked investors flee and future returns are best, and it removes the paralysis of waiting for the perfect entry point. Set up the automatic transfer, pick a broad index fund, and let the schedule quietly build your holdings month after month through every market mood.
