# Diversifying your investments

Diversification is the practice of spreading your savings across many different holdings so that no single company, sector, or asset can sink your whole portfolio. The idea captures the old wisdom of not putting all your eggs in one basket: when one holding stumbles, others may hold steady or rise, smoothing the overall ride. A well-diversified household portfolio owns stocks across many industries and countries, blends them with bonds, and avoids concentrating too much in any one employer's stock or a single hot ticker. Broad mutual funds and index funds deliver instant diversification because a single share already holds hundreds of underlying companies. Diversification does not promise gains or eliminate every loss, but it removes the uncompensated risk of betting the family's future on one name. For most savers the practical takeaway is simple: own a little of everything, rebalance occasionally, and resist the urge to overload on last year's winner.
