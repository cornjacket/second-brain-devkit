# Insurance every household should consider

Insurance protects a household's finances by transferring the risk of a costly disaster to an insurer in exchange for a regular premium, so a single misfortune does not wipe out your savings. Health insurance shields you from catastrophic medical bills, while auto and homeowners or renters policies cover damage, theft, and liability to your property and vehicle. Term life insurance replaces a breadwinner's income if they die during the covered years, which matters most when children or a mortgage depend on that paycheck; it is far cheaper than whole life for the same protection. Disability insurance, often overlooked, replaces part of your wages if illness or injury keeps you from working. When choosing coverage, weigh the premium against the deductible, the amount you pay out of pocket before benefits kick in, and buy enough coverage to protect against ruin rather than every small inconvenience. Review policies as your family and assets grow.
