# Income tax basics for households

Understanding a few tax fundamentals helps a household keep more of its take-home pay legally. Your income is taxed in brackets, meaning only the dollars that fall within each tier are taxed at that tier's rate, so earning a bit more never drops your whole paycheck into a higher rate. The standard deduction reduces your taxable income automatically, and most filers take it rather than itemizing deductions like mortgage interest and charitable gifts. Tax credits are more valuable than deductions because they cut your tax bill dollar for dollar; the child tax credit and earned income credit are common household examples. Money you put into a traditional 401k or IRA lowers this year's taxable income, while contributing to a Roth trades that break for tax-free withdrawals later. Adjust your W-4 withholding so you neither owe a big bill nor hand the government an interest-free loan through a large refund, and file by the April deadline to avoid penalties.
