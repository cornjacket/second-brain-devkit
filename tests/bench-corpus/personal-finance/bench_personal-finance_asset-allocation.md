# Choosing your asset allocation

Asset allocation is how you divide your investment savings among the major categories of stocks, bonds, and cash, and it is the single biggest driver of a household portfolio's long-run behavior. Stocks offer the highest growth but swing hardest, bonds add stability and income, and cash provides safety and liquidity, so the mix you pick sets your expected return and how bumpy the journey feels. A common starting rule of thumb subtracts your age from 110 or 120 to suggest the stock percentage, tilting younger savers toward growth and shifting retirees toward safer bonds as their time horizon shortens. Because markets move unevenly, your allocation drifts over time, so rebalancing periodically by selling a little of what grew and buying what lagged restores your target weights and quietly enforces buy-low, sell-high discipline. Match the allocation to your goals and time horizon, then let it ride through the noise rather than chasing performance.
