# Setting up an emergency fund

An emergency fund is a pool of readily accessible cash set aside to cover unexpected household expenses, so a surprise car repair, medical bill, or sudden job loss does not force you onto a credit card or into debt. The common target is three to six months of essential living expenses, meaning rent or mortgage, groceries, utilities, insurance premiums, and minimum debt payments rather than your full lifestyle spending. Households with variable income or a single earner often stretch toward the higher end. Keep this money separate from your everyday checking so you are not tempted to spend it, ideally in a high-yield savings account where it stays liquid and still earns a little interest. Build it gradually with automatic transfers each payday, starting with a modest starter cushion of a thousand dollars, then topping it up. Replenish the fund promptly after any withdrawal so your financial safety net stays intact.
