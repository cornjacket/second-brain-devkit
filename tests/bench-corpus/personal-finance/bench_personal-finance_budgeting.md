# Building a monthly household budget

A budget is a written plan that assigns every dollar of your monthly take-home pay to a spending category before the month begins, so your paycheck works on purpose instead of vanishing. Many households use the 50/30/20 guideline, steering roughly half of net income toward needs like rent, groceries, and utilities, thirty percent toward wants, and twenty percent toward savings and extra debt payments. Zero-based budgeting goes further, giving every dollar a job until income minus outgo equals zero. The envelope method splits cash or virtual buckets by category so overspending in one line is visible immediately. Track discretionary spending against these category limits weekly, reconcile against your checking account, and roll unspent amounts forward. A budget is not restriction; it is telling your money where to go so your grocery, dining, and subscription outflows finally align with your household priorities and take-home pay.
