# The Cardiovascular System and Circulation

The cardiovascular system circulates blood to deliver oxygen and nutrients and remove metabolic wastes throughout the organism. The four-chambered heart drives a double circulation: the right ventricle pumps deoxygenated blood through the pulmonary circuit to the lungs for gas exchange, while the left ventricle propels oxygenated blood through the systemic circuit at higher pressure. Cardiac muscle contracts rhythmically under the intrinsic pacemaker of the sinoatrial node, whose electrical impulse spreads through the atrioventricular node and Purkinje fibers to coordinate the cardiac cycle of systole and diastole. Arteries with elastic muscular walls carry blood away from the heart, arterioles regulate resistance and blood pressure, and thin capillary beds permit diffusion of gases and solutes across their single endothelial layer. Venules and veins return blood via one-way valves and skeletal muscle pumps. Erythrocytes bearing hemoglobin transport oxygen, while plasma carries dissolved carbon dioxide as bicarbonate, sustaining tissue perfusion and cellular respiration.
