# The Nervous System and Neural Signaling

The nervous system coordinates rapid communication through electrically excitable neurons that transmit signals as action potentials. A neuron comprises dendrites receiving input, a soma integrating it, and an axon conducting the impulse toward its terminals. At rest the plasma membrane maintains a negative resting potential established by the sodium-potassium pump and selective ion permeability. Depolarization past threshold opens voltage-gated sodium channels, generating an all-or-none action potential that propagates via saltatory conduction between nodes of Ranvier along myelinated axons. At the synapse the impulse triggers calcium influx and exocytosis of neurotransmitters into the synaptic cleft, where they bind postsynaptic receptors to excite or inhibit the next neuron. The central nervous system of brain and spinal cord integrates sensory afferents and motor efferents relayed by the peripheral nervous system, while the autonomic division governs involuntary sympathetic and parasympathetic responses. Reflex arcs enable rapid protective responses without conscious cortical processing.
