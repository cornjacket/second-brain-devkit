# Protein Synthesis Transcription and Translation

Protein synthesis expresses the genetic code through the coordinated processes of transcription and translation. During transcription RNA polymerase binds a promoter and synthesizes a complementary messenger RNA strand from the DNA template, and in eukaryotes the primary transcript is capped, polyadenylated, and spliced to excise introns and join exons. The mature mRNA exits the nucleus to the ribosome, where translation decodes its sequence in triplet codons. Transfer RNA molecules bearing specific anticodons and charged with cognate amino acids by aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases enter the ribosomal A site, and peptidyl transferase catalyzes peptide bond formation between adjacent residues. The ribosome translocates codon by codon from the start codon AUG until a stop codon triggers release factors to liberate the nascent polypeptide. The resulting amino acid chain folds into its native conformation, sometimes assisted by chaperones, adopting the secondary and tertiary structure that determines its biological function according to the central dogma.
