# Building character backstory

Building a character means giving an actor a life beyond the lines, and teachers coach the construction of a backstory that makes choices inevitable. Students answer the questions the script leaves open — the character's history, relationships, wants, fears, and the events that shaped them — so behavior flows from a coherent inner life rather than moment-to-moment invention. The discipline is to root every invention in the text and let it serve the role; backstory that contradicts the play, or that the actor indulges privately without it changing behavior, is wasted. Coaches connect this to objective and given circumstances so the biography feeds what the character wants right now. The test is whether the imagined history actually changes how the actor plays a line. A well-built character feels like a whole person caught in a particular hour.
