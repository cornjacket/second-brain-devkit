# Teaching on-camera acting

Screen acting is a distinct discipline, and teachers retrain stage actors whose performances are too big for the lens. The camera is intimate and reads thought, so the central lesson is to do less and let the truth happen behind the eyes; a flicker registers where a stage gesture would overwhelm. Students learn the technical demands that shape the work: hitting marks, matching action and eyelines across takes for continuity, and adjusting scale to the shot size, since a close-up needs far less than a wide. Coaches teach actors to stay fresh and spontaneous across many repeated takes and out-of-order shooting, and to keep a performance alive without a live audience's energy. The through-line is honesty and stillness — the camera rewards genuine, specific thought and punishes anything pushed or indicated.
