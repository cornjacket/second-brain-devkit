# Teaching ensemble and etiquette

Ensemble work teaches that theatre is collaborative, and studios instill it as both a skill and an ethic. Students learn to give and take focus — knowing when to drive a scene and when to support — so the storytelling stays clear and no one upstages the moment that matters. Teachers build trust through group exercises so actors will take risks and genuinely catch one another, and they establish rehearsal etiquette: being prepared and on time, being generous with scene partners, keeping choices consistent so others can rely on them, and respecting the director and the space. The lesson is that a scene belongs to the story, not to any single actor, and that a company that listens and supports each other produces far better work than a room of soloists competing for attention.
