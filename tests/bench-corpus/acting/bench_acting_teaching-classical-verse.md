# Teaching classical verse

Teaching classical text, especially Shakespeare, means giving actors tools to make heightened language playable and clear. Students learn to scan iambic pentameter and to use the meter as a guide — where the rhythm breaks or a line runs short signals emotional pressure — while never letting the verse turn sing-song. Teachers unpack the rhetoric: the antitheses, lists, and building arguments that structure a speech as active thought, and they insist actors ride the thought to the end of the line rather than chopping it into naturalistic fragments. Vocabulary and imagery are decoded so the actor truly understands every word before speaking it. The lesson is that the language is not an obstacle but the character's means of getting what they want, so verse technique serves the objective and the argument, not merely beautiful sound.
