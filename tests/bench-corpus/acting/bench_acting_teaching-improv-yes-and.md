# Teaching improv "yes, and"

Improvisation training rests on "Yes, and" — accepting whatever your partner offers as true and building on it — and teachers drill it because the instinct to block or deny kills a scene. Students learn to make and accept offers, to add new information rather than merely agreeing, and to treat their partner as a genius whose ideas they support. Exercises strip away the pressure to be clever, since the funniest and truest scenes come from committed, honest reactions rather than jokes. Teachers coach players to establish the who, what, and where quickly, to heighten a pattern once it emerges, and to listen far more than they plan. The through-line is generosity and presence: make your partner look good, and stay in the moment instead of writing the scene ahead in your head.
