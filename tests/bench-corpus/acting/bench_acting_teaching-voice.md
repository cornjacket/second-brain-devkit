# Teaching vocal technique

Vocal training gives an actor a flexible, healthy instrument, and teachers build it from the breath up. Students learn diaphragmatic breathing so the voice is supported by the body rather than squeezed from the throat, which protects it through eight shows a week. From there coaches develop resonance — placing the sound so it carries and fills a house without shouting — and articulation, drilling consonants and vowels so text stays intelligible at speed and at volume. Projection is taught as support and placement, not force. Releasing tension in the jaw, tongue, and neck removes what flattens tone and causes strain. Warm-ups and careful use prevent injury. The goal is an actor who can be heard in the back row, shift from a whisper to a roar, and speak demanding text without tiring or going hoarse.
