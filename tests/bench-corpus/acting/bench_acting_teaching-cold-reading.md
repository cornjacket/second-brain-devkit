# Teaching cold reading

Cold reading — performing a script you have barely seen — is a survival skill for auditions, and it can be taught as a method rather than left to nerves. Teach students to take a few seconds to scan for the essentials: who they are talking to, what they want, and the relationship, so a clear objective drives the read even without full preparation. The key physical skill is lifting words off the page in quick glances and delivering them to the reader, keeping the eyes up and connected during the important moments rather than buried in the sides. Coaches drill making bold, specific choices fast, since a committed choice reads better than a cautious blank. Students learn to keep going through stumbles and to stay genuinely responsive to the reader instead of reciting into the air.
