# Teaching script analysis

Script analysis is the actor's homework, and teachers train students to interrogate the text before making choices. They break a scene into beats — units of action that turn when the subject or tactic changes — and identify the character's objective in each, the super-objective across the whole play, and the through-line that connects them. Students learn to distinguish what a character says from what they want, and to hunt for clues in the punctuation, the imagery, and what other characters say about them. Coaches insist every choice be justifiable from the text, so analysis grounds the acting in evidence rather than whim. Done well, this preparation frees the actor: once the map of objectives and beats is clear, rehearsal becomes about living the scene rather than deciphering it line by line.
