# Teaching the Meisner repetition exercise

The Meisner repetition exercise is a foundational drill for teaching actors to work off impulse instead of intellect. Two students sit facing each other and repeat a simple observation back and forth — "you're smiling," "I'm smiling" — letting the phrasing shift naturally as real behavior emerges between them. The point is to get out of the head and onto the partner, so attention lives outside yourself and responses become truthful reactions rather than planned line readings. As the instructor, listen for the moment repetition stops being mechanical and starts carrying genuine emotional life, then coach the students to let that change the words. Meisner's mantra — that acting is living truthfully under imaginary circumstances — frames the whole approach. Later stages layer in preparation, independent activities, and knocks on the door to raise the stakes and test the actor's focus.
