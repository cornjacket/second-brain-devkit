# Teaching physical characterization

Physical characterization teaches actors to build a role through the body, not just the voice and intellect. A common framework is Laban's efforts, which describe movement by weight, time, space, and flow, giving students a vocabulary to make specific choices — a character who is heavy and sudden inhabits space differently from one who is light and sustained. Teachers have students explore a walk, a center of gravity, a habitual gesture, or an animal image until a distinct physical life emerges that is not the actor's own. The lesson is that changing how you move changes how you feel and how the audience reads the character. Coaches guard against generic clichés by demanding specificity, and they help the actor integrate the physical life so it supports the text and objective rather than decorating them.
