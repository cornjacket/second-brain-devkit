# Teaching comic technique

Comic technique can be taught, and good teachers demystify timing rather than treating it as an innate gift. The core lesson is that comedy grows from a character playing a situation with complete seriousness and high stakes, not from trying to be funny, so the actor commits fully to the objective and lets the absurdity land. Students learn the mechanics of setup and payoff, protecting the operative word so a joke's structure is not muffled, and the value of the pause and the reaction that let an audience catch up and laugh. Coaches drill precision — comedy is exact, and a moment too early or late can kill a laugh — and teach actors to ride and top a laugh without stepping on it. Truthful, specific, fully committed playing is what ultimately makes it funny.
