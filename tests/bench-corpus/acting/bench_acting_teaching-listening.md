# Teaching listening and reacting

The oldest note in the studio is that acting is reacting, and teaching genuine listening is harder than it sounds because students often wait for their cue instead of taking anything in. Teachers drill being in the moment: really watching and hearing the scene partner so each response is caused by what just happened rather than pre-planned. Exercises strip out the lines entirely, or force actors to respond only to the partner's changing behavior, so attention lives outside the self. Coaches point out the tells of faking it — the glazed look of someone reciting, the response that ignores what the partner just did — and push actors to let the partner visibly affect them. The lesson is that a scene is alive only when both actors are changed, moment to moment, by each other.
