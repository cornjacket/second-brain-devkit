# Teaching objectives and actions

A core lesson in an acting class is that a character always wants something, and the actor's job is to pursue that objective rather than to "show" an emotion. Teach students to phrase an objective as an active, playable verb aimed at the other person — to seduce, to warn, to shame — because a strong verb produces behavior, while a vague feeling produces indicating. Break a scene into beats, each with its own objective and the tactics the character tries when the last one fails. The obstacle is what stands in the way; the stakes are what happens if they fail. Coach actors to keep their attention on the scene partner so the pursuit stays alive and responsive instead of pre-planned. When a scene goes dead in class, the usual diagnosis is a weak or missing objective — sharpen the verb and it wakes up.
