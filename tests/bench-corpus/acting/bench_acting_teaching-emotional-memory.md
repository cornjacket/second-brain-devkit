# Teaching emotional memory

Emotional or affective memory is a technique — traceable to Stanislavski and central to American Method training — for reaching genuine feeling by recalling a personal experience with a similar emotional charge. Teachers guide students to reconstruct the sensory details of a past event, the sounds, smells, and textures, so the associated emotion arises on its own rather than being pushed or indicated. It is taught cautiously: reaching for raw personal trauma on demand can be unsafe and unreliable, so many coaches favor lighter substitutions or imagined alternatives that are equally specific but less costly. The point students must grasp is that the recalled feeling serves the scene's needs and stays controllable. Overused, it turns an actor inward and self-indulgent; used sparingly, it can unlock honest emotion that pretending never reaches.
