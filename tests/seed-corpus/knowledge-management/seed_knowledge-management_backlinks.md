# Backlinks

A backlink is the inverse of an outgoing wikilink: it surfaces every note that references the current one, so a plain reference silently becomes bidirectional. In Obsidian the backlinks pane lists both linked mentions (explicit `[[wikilink]]` targets) and unlinked mentions where your note's title appears as raw text, inviting you to promote it into a real link. Because the vault computes these automatically, you never maintain a manual "referenced by" section. Backlinks are the engine behind linking-your-thinking and the Zettelkasten's associative web: as you write daily notes and permanent notes, connections accrete without upfront taxonomy, and the graph view renders each backlink as an edge. Reviewing a note's backlinks reveals context you never explicitly recorded — the neighboring thoughts that cite it — which is where unexpected associations and emergent maps of content tend to appear.
