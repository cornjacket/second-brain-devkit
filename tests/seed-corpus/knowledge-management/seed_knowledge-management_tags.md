# Tags vs links

Tags and links are the two connective primitives in a vault, and the perennial PKM debate is when to reach for each. A `#tag` is a lightweight categorical label — a flat or nested classifier like `#status/draft` or `#area/health` that answers "what kind of note is this," letting you filter or query many notes by membership. A `[[wikilink]]`, by contrast, asserts a specific relationship between two individual notes and generates a backlink, weaving the associative graph that Zettelkasten and linking-your-thinking depend on. The common guidance is to link for ideas and tag for states or workflow status, because sprawling tag taxonomies decay into noise: near-synonyms multiply, and a tag with hundreds of members loses discriminating power. Many practitioners therefore keep a small, deliberately controlled tag vocabulary, lean on full-text search and backlinks for discovery, and reserve tags for cross-cutting attributes that links and maps of content cannot easily express.
