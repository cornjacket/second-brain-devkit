# The PARA method

PARA, from Tiago Forte's Building a Second Brain, is an organizing scheme that sorts every note into one of four top-level buckets — Projects, Areas, Resources, and Archives — by actionability rather than by subject. A Project is a short-term effort with a defined outcome and deadline; an Area is an ongoing responsibility you maintain to a standard with no end date; Resources are topics of reference interest you may draw on later; and Archives hold anything inactive from the other three. The defining move is that notes migrate between buckets as their actionability changes: a completed project's notes drop into Archives, and a resource can be pulled up when a new project needs it. Because PARA keys off "how soon will I act on this," it keeps the most relevant material closest to hand and resists the topic-tree paralysis of subject folders. It pairs naturally with frictionless capture — the inbox feeds triage, and triage assigns each note its PARA home.
