# Evergreen notes

Evergreen notes, a term from Andy Matuschak, are written to be developed, revised, and reused across many contexts rather than consumed once and discarded. Two principles define them: they are concept-oriented — titled by the idea, not by a source or date — and atomic, holding a single claim so the note becomes a reusable unit you can link precisely. The title is often phrased as a complete declarative assertion (an "API for your thinking") so it reads well inline as a `[[wikilink]]`. Unlike fleeting notes captured in the inbox or daily note, evergreen notes are never finished; you refactor them as understanding deepens, splitting overloaded notes to preserve atomicity and densely cross-linking them so backlinks and maps of content emerge bottom-up. This is the connective tissue of a Zettelkasten slip-box and the core of linking-your-thinking: knowledge that compounds because each revision and each new link makes the whole web more retrievable.
