# Maps of content

A map of content (MOC), a pattern popularized by Nick Milo's Linking Your Thinking, is a hand-curated hub note that gathers `[[wikilinks]]` to related notes on a theme, giving a vault navigable structure without committing ideas to a rigid folder tree. An MOC is an entry point and a workbench: you grow it as a topic matures, and it often nests — a high-level "home" or "index" MOC links down to narrower MOCs, which link to individual atomic and evergreen notes. Unlike a tag, an MOC is itself a note you can annotate, so it captures your evolving mental model of how ideas relate, not just a flat membership list. MOCs form emergently — you notice a cluster of backlinks accreting around a concept and lift it into a dedicated map. Because notes can appear in many MOCs at once, this associative indexing sidesteps the single-location constraint of deep folder hierarchies while keeping the graph view legible.
