# Zettelkasten

Zettelkasten — German for "slip-box" — is the note-taking method sociologist Niklas Luhmann used to author his prolific output from a physical box of index cards. Its unit is the permanent note (Zettel): a single atomic idea written in your own words, each assigned a stable identifier so it can be cited from anywhere. The workflow moves fleeting notes and literature notes (captured while reading) into permanent notes, which you then link densely to their conceptual neighbors rather than filing under topics. Luhmann threaded ideas with branching Folgezettel sequence IDs and a keyword index rather than folders, and modern implementations reproduce this with `[[wikilinks]]` and backlinks in a vault. The payoff is emergent: the slip-box becomes a "communication partner," and because notes accrete connections instead of sitting in categories, following the links surfaces unexpected juxtapositions that seed new writing. This bottom-up, link-first ethos underpins evergreen notes and linking-your-thinking.
