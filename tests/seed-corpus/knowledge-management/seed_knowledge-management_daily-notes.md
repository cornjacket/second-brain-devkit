# Daily notes

A daily note is a note titled by date (typically `YYYY-MM-DD`) that serves as the day's landing pad for capture — tasks, meeting scraps, fleeting thoughts, and quick wikilinks jotted before you decide where anything belongs. Obsidian's Daily Notes core plugin (and Periodic Notes for weekly and monthly variants) opens today's note on a hotkey from a template, so journaling has near-zero friction. The date itself becomes a stable backlink hub: mentioning `[[2026-07-09]]` from any note threads that note into the day's timeline, and the daily note's own backlinks pane reconstructs everything that referenced it. Daily notes are deliberately fleeting scratchpads, not permanent notes; during your review ritual you harvest the durable ideas, rewrite them as atomic evergreen notes, and link them out, leaving the daily note as a dated journaling trail rather than a home for lasting knowledge.
