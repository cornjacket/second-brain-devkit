# Frictionless capture

Frictionless capture holds that the first job of a note-taking practice is to get a fleeting thought out of your head and into the vault before it evaporates — filing and linking come later. Every point of friction (choosing a folder, picking tags, deciding atomicity) is a reason to not write it down, so you defer all of it. The pattern is a single trusted inbox: a global quick-capture hotkey or mobile share sheet drops raw fleeting notes into one place, appended to today's daily note or an inbox folder. Then you commit to an inbox-zero processing ritual — triage each capture, discard the noise, and refile the durable ideas into permanent or evergreen notes with proper wikilinks. This capture-first, organize-later separation is why a messy inbox you actually process beats an elaborate PARA hierarchy you avoid opening.
